Dining & Onboard Life

Alaska cruises are just as much about cozy evenings on the ship as they are about glaciers. A little planning helps you enjoy the food, avoid long lines, and still make it to the shows.

Dining Options You’ll Usually See

  • Main dining room with a rotating dinner menu
  • Buffet for breakfast, lunch, and casual dinners
  • Specialty restaurants (steakhouse, sushi, Italian, etc.)
  • Coffee shops and snack spots for quick bites
  • Room service for late-night snacks or lazy mornings

Tips to Eat Well Without Stress

  • Check the daily schedule and menus in the cruise app or paper newsletter.
  • Book specialty restaurants early on sea days if you want a specific time.
  • Plan earlier dinners on glacier or show nights so you’re not rushed.
  • Ask for a table by a window if ocean views are important to you.

Onboard Routines That Work

  1. Begin the day with breakfast and a quick check of the daily schedule.
  2. Block time for hot tubs, trivia, or live music between ports.
  3. Wind down with a warm drink on deck while watching the scenery.
