What to Pack for an Alaska Cruise

Alaska weather can swing from sunny and warm to cold and misty in a single day. The goal is layers so you can warm up or cool down quickly without overpacking.

Core Clothing Layers

  • 2–3 moisture-wicking base layer tops
  • 1–2 lightweight sweaters or fleeces
  • 1 warm jacket (down or synthetic)
  • 2 pairs of comfortable jeans or hiking pants
  • 1 pair of waterproof pants for glacier and rain days

Outerwear & Footwear

  • Waterproof rain jacket with a hood
  • Comfortable waterproof walking shoes or light hiking boots
  • Flip flops or sandals for the pool or spa
  • Warm hat, gloves, and a neck gaiter or scarf

Don’t Forget These Extras

  • Binoculars for whale and wildlife watching
  • Small daypack for port days and excursions
  • Refillable water bottle
  • Sunscreen and lip balm with SPF (yes, even in Alaska!)
  • Motion sickness remedies if you’re prone to sea sickness

Simple Packing Plan

  1. Lay out everything you think you need.
  2. Remove one third of the “just in case” outfits.
  3. Double-check that you have layers for warm, cool, and rainy days.
